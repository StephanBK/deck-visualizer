# Deck Construction Research Dossier (2026-07-08)

Source basis: AWC DCA-6 "Prescriptive Residential Wood Deck Construction Guide" (2015 IRC ed., read in full), Trex 2020 Decking Installation Guide, Trex Fascia Installation Recommendations, Simpson Strong-Tie Deck Connection & Fastening Guide, California Residential Code (CRC) handouts, County of San Diego PDS-044, Pro Builder / InspectAPedia ledger-flashing best practices.

**CRITICAL CALIFORNIA NOTE (verified):** 2022 CRC §R312 requires guard (railing) height of **42" minimum**, not the IRC's 36". Guards required whenever the walking surface is more than 30" above grade.

---

## Phase 1: Site Layout & Ledger Attachment

### What happens
Crew marks the deck footprint with stakes and string lines, squared off the house wall (3-4-5 triangle). Siding is cut away where the deck meets the house; a pressure-treated ledger board is bolted through the sheathing into the house rim joist. Metal flashing is tucked behind the siding and lapped over the ledger top.

### Why it matters (sales talking points)
- The ledger is the #1 failure point in deck collapses. DCA-6 prohibits nailing it — 1/2" lag screws or through-bolts, staggered in two rows, into the structural rim joist (never brick veneer, stucco alone, hollow block).
- Flashing is cheap insurance against rot — most rotted rim joists trace to decks built without it.
- Deck surface sits a step below the door threshold (best practice 1"–3" below finished floor; code allows ≤1-1/2" below the egress door threshold) so wind-driven rain can't wick under the door.

### Visual elements
Wall cross-section with siding removed at ledger zone; ledger flat against wall, bolts in two staggered rows; L-shaped flashing (up behind housewrap, over ledger, drip edge); threshold above with deck surface a step below; string lines/stakes.

### Specs/labels
- Ledger min 2x8 PT, depth ≥ joist depth; max 1/2" gap to sheathing
- 1/2" lag screws or through-bolts w/ washers, spacing per DCA-6 Table 5 (36" o.c. for ≤8' joist spans down to ~19–24" o.c. at 14–18')
- Bolt rows 2" min from edges, staggered; lags penetrate ≥1/2" beyond band joist; HDG or stainless only
- Flashing: corrosion-resistant metal min 0.019" (no aluminum against ACQ lumber)

## Phase 2: Footings

### What happens
Holes dug at each post location to undisturbed soil; concrete footings poured. San Diego: no frost line → 12" min below undisturbed grade (vs 36–48" in cold climates). Galvanized post-base anchor set so wood never touches concrete/soil.

### Why it matters
- Sized to load per DCA-6 Table 4 (mid-size deck typically 20–24" dia; e.g. 12' beam span / ≤14' joist span → 30" round or 26"x26" square, 13" thick). Undersized footings sink.
- San Diego advantage: 12" deep suffices (CRC R403.1.4, County PDS-044) — but soil must be undisturbed; footings within 5'-0" of house must bear at house-foundation elevation.
- Call 811 before digging.

### Specs/labels
2,500 psi concrete; 1,500 psf assumed soil bearing; footing top ~1" above grade; anchor e.g. Simpson ABU66Z.

## Phase 3: Posts & Beam

### What happens
PT 6x6 posts on footing anchors, plumbed, cut to height. Beam (2–3 ply 2x10/2x12) set on notched post seats or post-cap connectors — bearing on wood, never side-bolted. Diagonal knee braces at tall corner posts.

### Why it matters
- DCA-6 Figure 9 prohibits side-bolted beams — beam must sit ON the post (notched 6x6 + (2) 1/2" through-bolts, or approved cap).
- Seismic D/E: continuous load path footing → post base → post cap → beam → joists with galvanized connectors (ABU base, BC/CCQ cap, LUS hangers).
- 6x6 min post (never 4x4 under DCA-6), max height 14'.

### Specs/labels
Beam spans per Table 3A (2-2x10 ≈ 6'-6" @ 10' joists; 3-2x12 ≈ 10'-6" @ 12'); cantilever ≤ LB/4; ply nailing 2 rows 10d @ 16" o.c., splices over posts; hardware HDG G-185 or stainless (304/316 within 300 ft of salt water — coastal SD).

## Phase 4: Joists

### What happens
Joists run ledger → beam, typically 16" o.c.; joist hangers at ledger; bear on beam with hurricane clips; rim joist caps outer ends; blocking; hold-down tension devices tie the frame back to house floor framing.

### Why it matters
- 16" o.c. = no bounce + composite warranty (Trex requires ≤16" perpendicular, 12" diagonal). 2x8 DF ≈ 11'-1" span @ 16" o.c.; 2x10 ≈ 13'-7".
- Hangers 500–600 lb per joist; IRC R507.2.4 hold-downs ≥1,500 lb at 2+ locations (e.g. Simpson DTT2Z) — the code answer to decks pulling off houses.
- Joist-top butyl tape (Trex Protect) keeps water out of screw holes.

### Specs/labels
Overhang ≤ L/4; rim to each joist (3) 10d or (3) #10x3"; hanger depth ≥60% joist depth.

## Phase 5: Decking

### What happens
Boards laid perpendicular (or ≤45°) starting at the house, uniform gaps, 2 fasteners per joist or hidden clips in grooved composite; flush-cut at outer edge, ~1" overhang.

### Why it matters
- Engineered gaps: Trex 1/8"–3/16" side and end (wider when cold), 1/4"–1/2" at walls/posts; wood ~1/8". San Diego heat = expansion room or buckling.
- Every board bears on ≥3 joists, 2 fasteners per joist (DCA-6) — no loose, squeaky, cupped boards.

## Phase 6: Railing & Stairs

### What happens
4x4 guard posts through-bolted to rim/outside joists (never nailed), rails + balusters; stairs on 2x12 stringers to a concrete pad, identical risers, graspable handrail full length.

### Why it matters
- **California: 42" guards** (2022 CRC §R312) on surfaces >30" above grade; openings reject a 4" sphere; posts resist 200 lb — (2) 1/2" through-bolts + ≥1,800 lb hold-down anchors; posts ≤6' apart.
- Stairs: max riser 7-3/4" (±3/8" variation), tread ≥10", width ≥36", handrail 34–38", graspable 1-1/4"–2", light at top landing switched inside (R303.7).

### Specs/labels
Stair guard openings <4-3/8"; triangle at stair base <6"; cut stringer max span 6'-0" (solid 13'-3"), ≤18" o.c.; stair footing 12" deep; landing ≥36".

## Phase 7: Fascia & Finish

### What happens
Fascia screwed over rim joists (Trex: every 18", 3 screws per location, expansion gaps), post caps, skirting, seal/stain for wood, cleanup, final inspection.

### Why it matters
- Fascia shields end-grain; correct fastening prevents heat waviness.
- DCA-6 item 12: no use/occupancy until final inspection approval — permit + green tag protects resale.

## Southern California / San Diego summary

- No frost line: footings 12" min below undisturbed grade; free-standing decks exempt from frost-depth rules (CRC R403.1.4.1 exc.)
- Footings within 5' of house bear at house-foundation elevation
- Guards 42" (CRC) — stricter than national 36"
- Seismic D/E: continuous load path hardware + diagonal bracing expected by inspectors
- Coastal corrosion: stainless 304/316 within 300 ft of salt water; else HDG G-185 with ACQ lumber
- Termite zone: PT or redwood/cedar heartwood; treat field cuts (copper naphthenate)
- Permits: generally required >30" above grade (City IB-211 / County PDS-044 mirror DCA-6)
